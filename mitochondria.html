Outer Membrane & Porins
Smooth outer boundary containing large barrel-like VDAC Porin channels permeable to small metabolites.
Intermembrane Space (IMS)
Fluid space between outer & inner membrane where high Proton (H⁺) gradient accumulates during respiration.
Cristae (Folded Inner Membrane)
Deep finger/shelf-like folds extending into matrix, packed with ETC Complexes and ATP Synthase knobs!
Matrix & Circular mtDNA
Dense interior containing circular double-stranded mtDNA, 70S Ribosomes, and Krebs cycle enzymes.
Energy Preparation Mechanism
Step 1 • Substrate Entry
Pyruvate & Oℶ Pass VDAC Porins
Pyruvate & Oxygen pass through outer membrane porin pores into the mitochondrial matrix.
Step 2 • Matrix Oxidation
Krebs Cycle (Citric Acid Cycle)
Matrix enzymes oxidize Pyruvate → Acetyl-CoA, releasing COℶ and loading electron carriers (NADH & FADH⁚).
Step 3 • Cristae ETC
Electron Transport & H⁺ Pumping
Complexes I, III & IV on Cristae folds pump H⁺ protons into Crista lumens / IMS creating electrochemical gradient.
Step 4 • Chemiosmosis
ATP Synthase Molecular Rotor
Protons rush back into matrix through ATP Synthase knobs, turning F₁ rotor to phosphorylate ADP + Pᵢ → ATP!

• Class 9 NCERT Biology Fact: Mitochondria are known as the Powerhouses of the Cell because they generate chemical energy in the form of ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate).  • Double Membrane & Cristae: Outer membrane has VDAC Porins; Inner membrane is deeply folded into shelf-like Cristae to maximize surface area for ATP synthesis.  • Self-Replicating Organelle: Mitochondria contain their own circular double-stranded DNA (mtDNA) and 70S ribosomes, making their own proteins!  • Chemiosmosis: ATP Synthase functions like a microscopic rotary turbine powered by H⁺ proton flow back into the matrix.
