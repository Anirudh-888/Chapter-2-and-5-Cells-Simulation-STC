Golgi Apparatus – The Cell's Postal System

Flat stacked cisternae · Cis face receives · Trans face dispatches · Modifies, Sorts & Packages

Golgi Regions
▲ Trans Face (Shipping)
Top of stack · Faces plasma membrane · Dispatches vesicles for transport, secretion & lysosome formation
Medial Golgi (Middle)
Central cisternae · Where glycosylation & phosphorylation modifications occur
▼ Cis Face (Receiving)
Bottom of stack · Faces ER & nucleus · Receives incoming vesicles from Rough ER
Vesicle Types
From Rough ER → Cis
Incoming vesicles carrying newly synthesised proteins & lipids
Lysosome Vesicles
Leave trans face carrying digestive enzymes to form lysosomes
Secretory Vesicles
Release cargo outside cell by exocytosis at plasma membrane
Membrane Transport
Deliver lipids & proteins to the plasma membrane
Functions
✏️ Modifies
Adds sugar chains (glycosylation) & phosphate groups to proteins & lipids as they pass through cisternae.
🗂️ Sorts
Reads molecular address tags and routes each cargo to its correct destination.
📦 Packages into Vesicles
Wraps sorted cargo into membrane vesicles for delivery to:
Transport Secretion Lysosome Formation
  Golgi discovered by Camillo Golgi in 1898  |  Called the "Post Office of the Cell"  |  A Golgi stack has 5-8 cisternae  |  Cis face is closest to the ER and nucleus; Trans face faces the plasma membrane  |  Glycosylation occurs mainly in medial Golgi  |  Lysosomes are formed by budding from the trans face  |  Secretory cells like goblet cells have a very large Golgi apparatus
