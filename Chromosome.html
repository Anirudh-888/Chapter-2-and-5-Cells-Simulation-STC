Key Biological Fact
Chromatin and Chromosomes are made of the exact same material (DNA and proteins). The only difference is how tightly packed (coiled) they are inside the cell!
1. Chromatin (Non-Dividing)
In a cell that is not dividing, the DNA is loose and spread out. It appears as a tangled network of long thread-like structures called chromatin. This allows the cell to read genetic codes easily.
2. Nucleosomes (Beads-on-a-string)
If you zoom close into chromatin, you see the DNA double helix wrapping around clusters of 8 spherical histone proteins. These are called nucleosomes and look like beads on a string.
3. Chromosomes (Dividing)
When a cell prepares to divide, the chromatin thread coils up extremely tightly (condenses) into thick, rod-like structures called chromosomes. This prevents DNA from tangling or breaking during division.
4. Chromatids, Centromeres & Genes
  • Sister Chromatids: The two arms of a replicated chromosome.
  • Centromere: The central junction point holding chromatids together.
  • Genes: Functional sections of DNA that code for proteins, visible as stripes (gene bands) on chromosome arms.
