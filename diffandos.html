📖 scientific glossary
Diffusion: Solute particles move randomly from higher to lower concentration until evenly distributed (equilibrium).
Osmosis: Solvent (water) molecules diffuse through a selectively permeable membrane to a region of higher solute concentration.
Hypotonic: A solution with a lower solute concentration (more free water molecules). Water moves out of this area.
Hypertonic: A solution with a higher solute concentration (fewer free water molecules). Water moves into this area.
Selectively Permeable: A membrane that allows small molecules (like water) to pass through its pores while blocking larger solutes.
