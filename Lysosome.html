Lysosomal Functions
🔄 Autophagy
Lysosome fuses with autophagosomes to digest worn-out organelles and cellular debris using acid hydrolases.
Self-digestionRecycling
🦠 Phagocytosis
Fuses with phagosomes containing engulfed bacteria or foreign particles. Enzymes destroy pathogens at low pH.
DefenceImmunity
📦 Endocytosis
Fuses with endosomes to break down macromolecules (proteins, lipids, carbohydrates) taken in from outside.
NutrientDigestion
☠ Autolysis / Apoptosis
In damaged cells, lysosomes release enzymes causing self-destruction. Called the "suicide bags" of the cell.

Lysosomes
🔬 Lysosomes are single-membrane organelles  ·  ⚗️ They contain ~60 different acid hydrolase enzymes  ·  ⚗️ Optimal pH inside a lysosome: 4.5 – 5.0  ·  🔄 Autophagy: Digesting own damaged organelles for recycling  ·  🦠 Phagocytosis: Destroying engulfed bacteria and pathogens  ·  ☠️ Autolysis: Lysosomes rupture and digest the entire cell — "Suicide Bags" (term by de Duve)  ·  🏭 Lysosomes are produced by the Golgi apparatus  ·  🔬 Discovered by Christian de Duve in 1955  ·  🧬 Size: 0.1 – 1.2 micrometers in diameter  ·  💊 Lysosomal storage diseases: e.g., Tay-Sachs, Gaucher's, Pompe disease
