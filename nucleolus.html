Terminology Alert for Class 9
In exams, do not confuse Nucleolus with Nucleoid!
  • Nucleolus: The dense round factory inside a true, membrane-bound nucleus (found in complex Eukaryotes like plants and animals).
  • Nucleoid: An open, membrane-free region where genetic material floats in simple Prokaryotes (like bacteria).
1. The Nucleolus (Dense Round Body)
Located inside the nucleus, the nucleolus is a dense, round structure. It has no membrane of its own. It serves as a biological factory designed specifically to synthesize ribosomes.
2. Synthesis of Ribosomal Subunits
The nucleolus transcribes Ribosomal RNA (rRNA) and combines it with ribosomal proteins imported from the cytoplasm. It builds two separate halves: the Small Subunit and the Large Subunit.
3. Export through Nuclear Pores
Once constructed, the subunits exit the nucleus. They pass through small gates in the double-layered nuclear envelope called Nuclear Pores.
4. Cytoplasmic Assembly & Function
Once in the cytoplasm, the large and small subunits combine on a messenger RNA (mRNA) strand to build functional ribosomes. These ribosomes read code to synthesize essential proteins.
