Did You Know?
If you stretched all the DNA from a single human cell in a straight line, it would be about 2 meters long! It fits in the tiny cell nucleus because it coils around histone beads into chromatin, which tightly folds into chromosomes.
1. Chromosomes and DNA
A chromosome is a highly coiled package of DNA. On the left end of the simulation, you can see how a chromatid unwinds, loops around histone proteins, and stretches out into a long DNA double helix.
2. The Double Helix Structure
DNA is structured as a twisted ladder (a double helix). The two sides of the ladder are the sugar-phosphate backbones, and the rungs are made of nitrogenous base pairs.
3. Complementary Base Pairing
Bases follow strict pairing rules:
  • Adenine (A) always pairs with Thymine (T).
  • Guanine (G) always pairs with Cytosine (C).
They are held together by weak chemical bonds called hydrogen bonds.
4. What is a Gene?
A gene is a specific functional segment of the DNA molecule. Each gene contains instructions (a recipe) for the cell to build a specific protein, which controls our traits (like height, blood group, or eye color).
